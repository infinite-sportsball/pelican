Benches Clear After Hit Batter; Indians Answer With Eight-Single Barrage
Charles Nagy had already given up a single to Daulton and a two-run homer to Devon White when he threw his last pitch of the top of the seventh. It hit Craig Counsell square. Counsell charged the mound like a man who'd been waiting for that particular insult in some other life. The benches emptied, fists were considered, Nagy departed. And then Cleveland, as if the brawl had been less a disruption than a stage direction, hit eight consecutive singles off three Florida relievers in the bottom half and turned a one-run game into a massacre.

The Indians won 11-4, and if you want the shape of this forty-ninth October Tuesday, picture a polite dinner party where somebody flips the table - and the hosts calmly serve dessert. Cleveland had led 2-0 in the first on a David Justice double that split right field at 108 miles an hour and a wild pitch that even Leiter seemed to lose interest in chasing. Florida tied it in the fourth on back-to-back doubles by Eisenreich and Alou. The Indians inched ahead again, 5-2, on the kind of slow-drip rally built from doubles and aggressive baserunning - Thome lining one into the corner, Sandy Alomar scoring standing up while the throw went to the wrong base entirely.

Then Devon White deposited a 1-0 pitch 333 feet into the seats to make it 5-4, and for one at-bat the game had the shape of something Florida could steal. That shape lasted exactly one pitch - the one that hit Counsell and set off the fireworks. After the smoke cleared and the relievers arrived, Jay Powell and Dennis Cook gave up six runs on eight singles, none struck with particular fury, all of them landing just beyond someone's reach like rain through a colander. Vizquel singled. Roberts singled on an 0-2 count. Justice singled and two men came home. Alomar singled and another scored. Thome battled to a full count and singled to make it 11-4, and by then Jacobs Field sounded less like a ballpark than a dog track on a night the favorite was running away from the field and everybody already had the ticket.
