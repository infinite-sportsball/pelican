Boston 1986 Takes Title
The Boston 1986 Red Sox rolled over the Houston 1986 Astros 6-5 at Astrodome to easily win the 1986 World Series crown.

Boston 1986 won in a walk and swept the series from the Astros 1-0. It was their 1st championship title.

Winning manager Jim Smith praised his own club as well as the opposition, calling the Astros "a fine team."

"It wasn't as easy as it looked. We had to play good baseball to beat them," he said. "We always seemed to come up with the key hit at the right time and to make the plays on defense when we had to. That's how you win the World Series."

The Red Sox took 0th place in the Division 1 and rounded out the year with a 0-0 record.
