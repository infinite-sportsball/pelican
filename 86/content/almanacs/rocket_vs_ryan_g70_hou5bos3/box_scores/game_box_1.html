Houston 1986 Astros Take World Series
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 1986, as the Houston 1986 Astros topped the Boston 1986 Red Sox 5-3 tonight at Astrodome to win the World Series. It was the 1st title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said Houston 1986 starting pitcher Nolan Ryan, who was named series MVP.

The Astros took the series 1-0. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a 0th-place finish in the Division 1 and a 0-0 record.

Houston 1986 manager Jim Smith grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Ryan's words.

"Nolan said it best -- we played better than the other team," he said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
