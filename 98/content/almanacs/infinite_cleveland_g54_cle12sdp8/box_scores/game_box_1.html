Indians Defeat Padres
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 1998, as the Cleveland 1998 Indians topped the San Diego 1998 Padres 12-8 tonight at Jacobs Field to win the World Series. It was the 1st title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said Cleveland 1998 center fielder Kenny Lofton, who was named series MVP.

The Indians took the series 1-0. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a 0th-place finish in the Division 1 and a 0-0 record.

Cleveland 1998 manager Jim Smith grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Lofton's words.

"Kenny said it best -- we played better than the other team," he said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
