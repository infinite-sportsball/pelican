San Diego 1998 Padres Take World Series

Colon, Cleveland's starter, was getting his strikeouts and walking too many men, and falling behind in ways that Tony Gwynn does not forgive. Gwynn singled home Quilvio Veras in the third and doubled him home again in the fifth, and a smothered infield single by Ken Caminiti made it 3-0. The Jacobs Field crowd, settling into the eighty-ninth telling of this particular October Tuesday, began to make the particular noise of forty-five thousand people pretending they weren't worried.

It was the seventh that turned a manageable evening into a rout. Jim Leyritz singled. Wally Joyner doubled.
