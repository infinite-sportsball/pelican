Brooklyn 1956 Beats New York 1956 in World Series
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 1956, as the Brooklyn 1956 Dodgers topped the New York 1956 Yankees 11-6 tonight at Yankee Stadium I to win the World Series. It was the 1st title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said Brooklyn 1956 catcher Roy Campanella, who was named series MVP.

The Dodgers took the series 1-0. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a 0th-place finish in the Division 1 and a 0-0 record.

Brooklyn 1956 manager Jim Smith grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Campanella's words.

"Roy said it best -- we played better than the other team," he said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
