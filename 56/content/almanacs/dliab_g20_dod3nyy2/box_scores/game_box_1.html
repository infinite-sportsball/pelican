Brooklyn 1956 Dodgers Are Champs
This was a game of two monster home runs that changed nothing. Sandy Amoros hit one 471 feet for Brooklyn in the second; Yogi Berra answered with one 472 feet for New York in the fourth. After that, the game retreated into something smaller and tighter, a contest decided not by power but by the width of the strike zone and the steadiness of two pitchers who refused to leave. Both Sal Maglie and Don Larsen threw complete games, 132 and 146 pitches respectively, and between them allowed nine hits in a game that lasted three hours and fifteen minutes and could have gone either way until the final out.
