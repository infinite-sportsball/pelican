Dodgers Defeat Yankees
Forty-one runs were scored at Yankee Stadium on this eighth telling of the afternoon, and the game lasted five hours and fourteen minutes, which is the sort of duration usually associated with doubleheaders or transatlantic crossings. The final score was 26-15, and the line score — 1, 0, 0, 0, 5, 4, 4, 3, 9 over 0, 1, 0, 4, 1, 0, 0, 6, 3 — reads less like a baseball game than like a conversation between two people who have forgotten how to stop talking. Both teams combined for 36 hits, 30 walks, and a quantity of pitching so bad it would take a statistician to catalog and a poet to forgive.

Robinson singled home Reese in the first to give Brooklyn a 1-0 lead, but Martin's sacrifice fly tied it in the second, and then the Yankees hammered Maglie for four in the fourth — Slaughter singling, Martin singling, McDougald walking, Carey singling, runners crossing the plate in a steady procession — until it was 5-1 and Larsen looked like a man who might actually survive the afternoon. He did not. In the fifth, Brooklyn sent ten batters to the plate and scored five runs on four hits and three walks, Furillo's two-out single through the left side bringing home Robinson and Hodges to put Brooklyn ahead 6-5. Collins answered immediately with a solo home run that traveled 458 feet to tie it at six, and after that the game abandoned any pretense of structure and became a kind of open wound, both teams scoring in great hemorrhaging clusters. Brooklyn retook the lead with four in the sixth and never looked back.

Brooklyn piled on four more in the seventh, three in the eighth, and nine in the ninth — a ninth inning in which Elston Howard walked five batters and hit another, and in which the Dodgers batted around twice. The Yankees, for their part, hit three consecutive home runs off Rube Walker in the eighth — Mantle a three-run shot on a full count, then Berra, then Slaughter, bang bang bang, 391 feet and 377 and 415 — and scored six runs in the inning without ever making the score feel close. Furillo went 5-for-6 with six RBI. Robinson scored five times. The game ended at dusk, the October light long since gone, and whatever it was that had happened at Yankee Stadium that afternoon, it was not the game that history had promised anyone.
