Brooklyn 1956 Triumphs in World Series
There was a moment in the bottom of the first inning, with two out and nobody on, when Mickey Mantle turned on an 0-1 pitch from Brooklyn starter Sal Maglie and drove it 419 feet into the rain beyond the right-center-field fence at Yankee Stadium, and for perhaps three minutes the sixty-four thousand believed this version of the afternoon belonged to New York. Mantle circled the bases in that unhurried trot of his, the ball still rolling somewhere in the bleacher seats, and the sound in the old ballpark had the particular warmth of a crowd that thinks it has seen the tone of a game established.
